## Assessment Summary (30% of module)

**Task:** Make a 5-minute science communication video in groups of 4.

**Structure:**
1. Teaser (~10s) — grab attention
2. Introduction to the topic and phenomenon
3. Main content: physics derivation + application
4. Interview with a real researcher
5. Conclusions

**Marking (100%):**
- 25% — Coverage (all required elements present, ~5 minutes)
- 50% — Content (scientific accuracy, selection of material, derivation quality)
- 25% — Presentation (editing quality, coherence, engagement, B-roll use)

**Deadline:** Friday Week 11 (end of term)

**Researchers available to interview:**
- Prof. Nick Stone (Exeter) — Raman spectroscopy for cancer diagnosis
- Dr. Ben Gardner (Exeter) — Biomedical Raman spectroscopy
- Dr. Sarah Mosca (Oxford/Harwell) — Raman analysis of artwork and preserved tissues
- Dr. Batom (Australia) — Vibrational spectroscopy for archaeological samples (mummies)
- Ruby (Exeter, final year) — AI for nonlinear optics cancer diagnostics

**Topics:** Cover one of the nonlinear/vibrational techniques from the course (Raman, IR, SHG, CARS, 2PEF, SRS, etc.), include a mathematical derivation, and link to real-world research.
