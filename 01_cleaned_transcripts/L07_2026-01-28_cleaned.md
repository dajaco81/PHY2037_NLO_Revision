## Infrared Absorption

### Selection Rule

> **[RED BOX]**

**For IR absorption to occur: the molecular vibration must result in a change in dipole moment.**

Physically: if the distribution of charge across the molecule changes as it vibrates, the photon can couple to it and be absorbed.

### Quantised Energy Levels

Treating the vibrating molecule as a quantum harmonic oscillator:

$$E_n = \left(n + \frac{1}{2}\right)\hbar\omega_{vib}$$

Absorption occurs when the photon energy matches the vibrational energy gap:

$$\Delta E = \hbar\omega_{vib} = h\nu_{vib}$$

### Diatomic Molecule: Calculating IR Absorption Frequency

Two masses mв‚Ѓ and mв‚‚ connected by a spring of constant k. Reduced mass:

$$\mu_r = \frac{m_1 m_2}{m_1 + m_2}$$

Vibrational frequency:

$$\nu_{vib} = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{k}{\mu_r}}$$

**Example calculation: C=O double bond**

$$m_C = \frac{12 \text{ g/mol}}{6.022 \times 10^{23}} = 1.993 \times 10^{-26} \text{ kg}$$

$$m_O = \frac{16 \text{ g/mol}}{6.022 \times 10^{23}} = 2.657 \times 10^{-26} \text{ kg}$$

$$\mu_r = \frac{m_C m_O}{m_C + m_O} = \frac{12 \times 16}{12 + 16} \times \frac{10^{-3}}{6.022 \times 10^{23}} = 1.139 \times 10^{-26} \text{ kg}$$

For a C=O double bond, k в‰€ 10 Г— 10ВІ N/m = 1000 N/m:

$$\nu_{vib} = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{1000}{1.139 \times 10^{-26}}} \approx 4.72 \times 10^{13} \text{ Hz}$$

### Wavenumber Units (cmвЃ»В№)

In vibrational spectroscopy, **wavenumber** is the standard unit:

$$\tilde{\nu} = \frac{\nu}{c} = \frac{1}{\lambda}$$

Expressed in **cmвЃ»В№** (inverse centimetres). The full vibrational fingerprint region spans ~0вЂ“4000 cmвЃ»В№.

Converting the C=O example: $\tilde{\nu}_{C=O} \approx 1570$ cmвЃ»В№

**Advantage:** All biologically relevant IR peaks fit on a 0вЂ“3500 cmвЃ»В№ scale with good precision (1вЂ“2 cmвЃ»В№).

## FTIR Imaging

IR microscopy produces a **hyperspectral data cube**: at each pixel (x, y) you record a full absorption spectrum (the z dimension). This gives you chemical maps showing the spatial distribution of every IR-active molecular species.

**Applications:** Label-free imaging of tissue sections, drug molecules in skin, cancer diagnosis.

**Limitations of IR imaging:**
1. **Poor spatial resolution**: d в€ќ О»; at 6.4 Вµm wavelength в†’ spatial resolution limited to several Вµm
2. **Water absorption**: water is a very strong IR absorber вЂ” cannot image hydrated (live) tissue easily
3. **Glass is IR-absorbing**: must use reflective (mirror-based) objectives вЂ” expensive, limited NA
4. **Thermal background**: everything in the room emits IR в†’ detector must be cryogenically cooled
5. **No depth selectivity**: standard IR is not confocal

## Raman Scattering

An alternative to IR absorption that overcomes the spatial resolution problem by using **visible excitation**.

### Physical Origin

Raman scattering is **inelastic scattering** of light from molecular vibrations:
- Incident photon at frequency П‰_L interacts with the molecule
- The molecule is in a certain vibrational state
- The scattered photon emerges at П‰_L В± П‰_vib (shifted by the vibrational frequency)
- The frequency shift reveals the molecular vibrational frequency

## IR vs. Raman: Comparison

| Property | IR Absorption | Raman Scattering |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Selection rule | Change in dipole moment | Change in polarizability |
| Excitation wavelength | IR (must match vib. frequency) | Any (typically visible) |
| Spatial resolution | Poor (Вµm scale, limited by О»_IR) | Good (visible О» в†’ ~300вЂ“500 nm) |
| Water compatibility | Poor (water absorbs strongly in IR) | Good |
| Signal strength | Strong (resonant process) | Very weak (~10вЃ¶ Г— weaker than fluorescence) |
| Glass optics | Not possible | Yes |
| 3D imaging | No (no depth selectivity) | Yes (with confocal detection) |
| Depth penetration in tissue | Very limited | Limited (but better than IR) |

**Bottom line:** Raman's weakness (low signal) means ~1вЂ“10 s per pixel в†’ too slow for imaging large areas or living tissue. This is why coherent Raman (CARS, SRS) was developed вЂ” nonlinear optics enhances the signal dramatically.

> *"The spectral complementarity of IR and Raman is useful: some modes active in one are inactive in the other. Machine learning can extract chemistry from both spectra."*
