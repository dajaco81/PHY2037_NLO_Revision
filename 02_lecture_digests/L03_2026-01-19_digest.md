# L03 Digest — Optical Imaging Part 1: Contrast Mechanisms

## Core Concepts

1. **Three main optical contrast mechanisms:**
   - **Absorption:** wavelength-selective removal of photons; colours arise from molecular electronic transitions
   - **Scattering:** wavelength-dependent redirection of photons; Rayleigh scattering (I ∝ 1/λ⁴)
   - **Fluorescence:** absorption followed by re-emission at longer wavelength (Stokes-shifted)

2. **Physical origin of molecular colour:** Electrons delocalised over conjugated π-bond systems (alternating C=C bonds). The length L of the conjugated system determines absorption wavelength (from infinite square well: λ ∝ L²).

3. **Jablonski diagram:** visual representation of electronic energy levels, vibrational sublevels, and optical transitions. Must be able to draw and label.

4. **Fluorescence:** ground state → excitation to S₁ → vibrational relaxation (heat, picoseconds) → emission at longer wavelength (Stokes shift).

5. **Labelling:** immunofluorescence (antibody + fluorophore) gives chemical specificity. H&E staining is the gold standard for cancer histopathology.

6. **Key limitation of labelling:** perturbation scales with molecular weight ratio. Fluorescent tags acceptable for large proteins (~10,000–100,000 g/mol); unacceptable for small molecules like drugs or lipids (~100–500 g/mol) — "transit van on an elephant".

| Equation | Name |
|----------|------|
| A = log(I₀/I_T) = εcl | Beer-Lambert law (absorbance) |

## Definitions

- **Absorbance A:** dimensionless quantity, log of the ratio of incident to transmitted intensity
- **Extinction coefficient ε:** intrinsic absorption property of the molecule (M⁻¹ cm⁻¹)
- **Stokes shift:** difference in wavelength between excitation and emission in fluorescence; arises because energy is lost to vibrational relaxation before emission
- **Jablonski diagram:** energy level diagram showing electronic states (solid thick lines), vibrational levels (thin lines), optical transitions (solid arrows), and non-radiative transitions (wavy arrows)

## Physical Intuition

Molecular colour arises from quantum mechanics: electrons confined in a π-conjugated chain behave like particles in a box. The longer the chain, the closer the energy levels, the longer the wavelength absorbed. This is why dyes are carefully engineered — changing the length of the conjugated system shifts the colour.

Fluorescence works because the molecule absorbs at one wavelength, quickly loses a little energy to vibration (heat), then emits at a longer wavelength. The time delay is nanoseconds for fluorescence, milliseconds for phosphorescence.

## Common Mistakes

1. Drawing wavy arrows as optical transitions on the Jablonski diagram (they are non-radiative/vibrational)
2. Forgetting that Beer-Lambert absorbance = log(I₀/I), not log(I/I₀)
3. Confusing fluorescence (ns timescale) with phosphorescence (ms timescale)
4. Saying fluorescence emission is at the same wavelength as absorption — always red-shifted (Stokes shift)
