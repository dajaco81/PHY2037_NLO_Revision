# L02 Digest — Prerequisite Physics Recap

## Core Concepts

1. **Electric polarisation:** P = ε₀χE links the applied field to the induced dipole distribution in a material. χ is frequency-dependent — this is the origin of all dispersive and absorptive optical phenomena.

2. **Displacement field D = ε₀E + P** separates free charge from bound charge; used later to write the nonlinear wave equation.

3. **Wave equation** (from Maxwell's equations): ∇²E = (1/c²)∂²E/∂t² — confirms free space supports EM wave propagation at speed c.

4. **Intensity:** I = ½cε₀|E|² — scales with electric field squared. Critical for nonlinear optics where signal scales with I² or I³.

5. **Harmonic oscillator resonance:** charges driven at their resonant frequency ω₀ give maximum displacement (90° phase lag). Below resonance: in-phase. Above resonance: 180° out of phase (no response).

6. **Atomic field strength:** E_at ≈ 5×10¹¹ V/m — the electric field an electron experiences in a hydrogen atom. Nonlinear effects become significant when the applied field approaches this value.

7. **Hydrogen absorption:** ΔE = 10.2 eV for n=1→2 transition → λ = 122 nm (extreme UV). Not useful for imaging — everything absorbs UV.

8. **Infinite square well:** L ≈ 0.67 nm needed for visible absorption — larger than an atom, so optical absorption in the visible requires electrons delocalised over multiple conjugated bonds.

9. **FWHM of Gaussian:** FWHM = 2√(2ln2)·σ ≈ 2.355σ

10. **Fourier transform of Gaussian is a Gaussian:** inversely related widths. Broader in time → narrower in frequency; shorter in time → broader in frequency (matches uncertainty principle).

## Derivations

### Bohr Radius
Balance centripetal force with Coulomb force, apply quantised angular momentum mvr = nℏ:
$$a_0 = \frac{4\pi\varepsilon_0\hbar^2}{me^2} \approx 0.53 \text{ Å}$$

### Hydrogen n=1→2 wavelength
ΔE = 13.6(1 − 1/4) = 10.2 eV → λ = hc/ΔE ≈ 122 nm (extreme UV)

### FWHM from standard deviation
At FWHM: I = I₀/2 → ½ = e^(−x²/2σ²) → x = σ√(2ln2) → FWHM = 2σ√(2ln2)

### Bandwidth conversion
f = c/λ → df/dλ = −c/λ² → |Δλ| = (λ²/c)|Δf|

## Physical Intuition

- **Harmonic oscillator analogy:** Charges in a material behave like masses on springs driven by the oscillating electric field of light. At resonance, maximum energy transfer; well above or below resonance, little response.
- **Why atoms are too small:** Visible light absorption requires electrons confined over ~0.67 nm — atoms are ~0.1 nm. Solution: conjugated molecular systems.
- **Fourier transform insight:** A perfectly monochromatic (single frequency) wave must exist for infinite time. Any time-limited pulse must contain multiple frequencies. Shorter pulse = broader frequency spectrum.

## Common Mistakes

1. Forgetting λ²/c factor when converting frequency bandwidth to wavelength bandwidth
2. Using atomic units inconsistently (e.g., wavenumber in cm⁻¹ but wavelength in nm without converting)
3. Confusing standard deviation σ with FWHM
