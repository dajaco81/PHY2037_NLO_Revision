# L14 Digest — Time-Bandwidth Product and 2PEF

## Core Concepts

1. **FWHM of intensity Gaussian:** I(t) ∝ exp(−t²/σ²) → FWHM = 2σ√ln2 (differs from amplitude FWHM by √2 factor due to squaring).

2. **Time-bandwidth product (TBP):**
   $$\Delta\nu \cdot \Delta t = \frac{2\ln 2}{\pi} \approx 0.441$$
   Valid for Gaussian-shaped transform-limited pulses. Derived from Fourier transform of Gaussian.

3. **Transform-limited pulse:** has the minimum duration for its bandwidth (no excess chirp). TBP = 0.441 is the lower bound.

4. **Physical picture of pulse formation:** superposition of many wavelengths; when all peaks coincide → maximum constructive interference → pulse; when phases diverge → destructive interference → zero between pulses.

5. **Two-photon excited fluorescence (2PEF):** I² dependence → signal only at focal spot → intrinsic confocality → no pinhole needed → deeper tissue penetration (16× less scattering at 2× wavelength).

## Derivations

### Time-Bandwidth Product for Gaussian Pulses

> **[RED BOX — must be able to derive]**

1. Amplitude pulse: E(t) = E₀exp(−t²/2σ_t²)
2. Fourier transform (given if needed): Ẽ(ν) ∝ exp(−2π²ν²σ_t²)
3. Convert amplitude to intensity: I(t) ∝ exp(−t²/σ_t²); I(ν) ∝ exp(−4π²ν²σ_t²)
4. FWHM_t: set I = I₀/2: exp(−t²/σ_t²) = ½ → Δt = 2σ_t√(ln2)
5. FWHM_ν: set I = I₀/2: exp(−4π²ν²σ_t²) = ½ → Δν = √(ln2)/(πσ_t)
6. Product: Δν·Δt = 2ln2/π ≈ 0.441

**Application:** Given Δt = 1 ps → Δν = 0.441/10⁻¹² = 441 GHz → Δλ = (λ²/c)×441 GHz

### Why Nonlinear Microscopy Works for Deep Tissue

For 1PEF (blue excitation at λ):
- Confocal with pinhole: scattered photons miss pinhole → lose signal
- Must increase laser power → photodamage
- Typical depth limit: 50–100 µm

For 2PEF (IR excitation at 2λ):
- Signal ∝ I² → only generated at focus → no pinhole needed
- Scattering of excitation: (2λ)⁻⁴ vs λ⁻⁴ → 16× less scattering
- Emitted fluorescence can scatter freely → collect on large-area detector
- Depth: up to 800–1000 µm demonstrated

## Physical Intuition

**Pulse as interference:** Imagine six tuning forks at slightly different frequencies, all struck simultaneously. At t=0 they're all in phase → loud sound. After a short time they drift out of phase → silence. At t = T (period) they're back in phase → another loud burst. The shorter the burst (pulse), the more forks (frequencies) you need.

**Why 2PEF is intrinsically confocal:** I₂PEF ∝ I². The PSF of intensity already falls off quickly from the focus. When you square it, it falls off even faster. The tails of the hourglass-shaped beam are dim — the square of dim is essentially zero. Signal is confined to the focal point without any need for a pinhole.

## Common Mistakes

1. Using the amplitude FWHM instead of the intensity FWHM (the factor of √2 matters)
2. Not converting Δν to Δλ correctly (same mistake as L12)
3. Saying 2PEF has worse resolution than 1PEF without mentioning the 1/√2 nonlinear squeeze
