# L16 Digest — CARS and SRS: Coherent Raman Microscopy

## Core Concepts

1. **CARS (Coherent Anti-Stokes Raman Scattering):** χ⁽³⁾ four-wave mixing process: 2ω_P − ω_S = ω_AS. Tune ω_P − ω_S = ω_vib. Coherent driving of molecular vibrations → enhanced Raman signal → video-rate imaging possible.

2. **SRS (Stimulated Raman Scattering):** same two-laser input (pump + Stokes at ω_P, ω_S with ω_P − ω_S = ω_vib). Stimulated emission at ω_S (gain) / pump depletion at ω_P (loss). Detected via modulation transfer with lock-in amplifier.

3. **CARS advantage over spontaneous Raman:** coherent excitation → ~N-fold enhancement (N = number of molecules in focal volume).

4. **CARS disadvantage:** non-resonant electronic background (all materials contribute to 4WM via electrons → background in every pixel).

5. **SRS advantage over CARS:** background-free (non-parametric process; only generates signal at vibrational resonance; no 4WM background).

6. **Pulse requirement:** For spectroscopic Raman (resolving individual peaks): picosecond excitation. For maximum peak intensity (2PEF/SHG/THG): femtosecond excitation.

## Derivations

### CARS Wavelength Calculation

> **[RED BOX — must be able to calculate]**

Given: λ_P = 800 nm, ν̃_vib = 2900 cm⁻¹ (CH₂ stretch)

**Stokes wavelength:**
$$\frac{1}{\lambda_S} = \frac{1}{\lambda_P} - \tilde{\nu}_{vib}$$

Convert to consistent units (cm): 1/λ_P = 1/(800×10⁻⁷ cm) = 12,500 cm⁻¹

$$\frac{1}{\lambda_S} = 12500 - 2900 = 9600 \text{ cm}^{-1} \implies \lambda_S = \frac{1}{9600} \text{ cm} \approx 1042 \text{ nm}$$

**Anti-Stokes wavelength:**
$$\frac{1}{\lambda_{AS}} = 12500 + 2900 = 15400 \text{ cm}^{-1} \implies \lambda_{AS} \approx 649 \text{ nm}$$

**Sanity check:** λ_S > λ_P (Stokes is red-shifted) ✓; λ_AS < λ_P (anti-Stokes is blue-shifted) ✓

## Physical Intuition

**CARS beating field:** Two lasers at ω_P and ω_S beat together, creating a modulation at ω_P − ω_S. If this matches ω_vib, the beat field drives the molecular vibration coherently (like pushing the swing at exactly the right frequency). All molecules in the focal volume are set vibrating in phase. When the pump photon comes in again, it stimulates the anti-Stokes emission coherently from all N molecules.

**Why SRS has no background:** In CARS the 4WM process generates a new photon at ω_AS even for non-resonant (electronic) contributions. In SRS you're measuring depletion of the existing pump beam — the pump can only lose energy to a vibrational mode, not to electronic transitions (different momentum conservation). So electronic background doesn't appear in SRS.

**Lock-in amplifier:** Modulate pump or Stokes at frequency f_mod. Mix the detected signal with a reference at f_mod. Low-pass filter. Output is the amplitude of signal component exactly at f_mod. Extracts tiny modulation (10⁻⁶ of signal) from a large background.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Unit mismatch** — wavenumber in cm⁻¹ but wavelength in nm (can't just subtract)
2. Getting Stokes and anti-Stokes backwards (Stokes is red-shifted/longer λ; anti-Stokes is blue-shifted/shorter λ)
3. Forgetting to account for non-resonant background when interpreting CARS images
4. Using femtosecond pulses for CARS and losing spectral selectivity
