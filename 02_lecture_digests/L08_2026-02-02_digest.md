# L08 Digest — Nonlinear Susceptibilities and First NLO Processes

## Core Concepts

1. **Power series:** P = ε₀[χ⁽¹⁾E + χ⁽²⁾E² + χ⁽³⁾E³ + …] Each order treated separately.

2. **χ estimates at E = E_at:**
   - χ⁽²⁾ ≈ 1/E_at ≈ 2×10⁻¹² m/V
   - χ⁽³⁾ ≈ 1/E_at² ≈ 4×10⁻²⁴ m²/V²

3. **Units of χ:** χ⁽¹⁾ dimensionless; χ⁽²⁾ in m/V; χ⁽³⁾ in m²/V². Each extra order of E adds one factor of (V/m)⁻¹.

4. **Centrosymmetry kills χ⁽²⁾:** For a centrosymmetric material, P⁽²⁾ = ε₀χ⁽²⁾E². When E → −E, E² stays positive, but P must change sign (physical displacement). Contradiction → χ⁽²⁾ = 0.

5. **Origin of nonlinearity:** At high fields the harmonic (parabolic) approximation for the restoring force breaks down → anharmonic potential → non-sinusoidal P response → new frequency components generated.

6. **SHG:** E + E → 2ω. Two photons in at ω, one out at 2ω. Quadratic in E → requires χ⁽²⁾ ≠ 0 → non-centrosymmetric material only.

7. **SFG/DFG:** Two fields ω₁ + ω₂ → new terms at ω₁+ω₂ (SFG), ω₁−ω₂ (DFG), 2ω₁, 2ω₂, and DC.

8. **THG (third order):** 3ω₁. Plus 44 total frequency components from three-field cubic mixing. The key one: 2ω₁ − ω₂ → basis of CARS.

## Derivations

### χ Estimate at E = E_at

Assumption: when E = E_at, P⁽¹⁾ ≈ P⁽²⁾:
$$\varepsilon_0\chi^{(1)}E_{at} \approx \varepsilon_0\chi^{(2)}E_{at}^2$$
$$\chi^{(2)} \approx \frac{\chi^{(1)}}{E_{at}} \approx \frac{1}{5\times10^{11}} \approx 2\times10^{-12} \text{ m/V}$$

**[RED BOX — method and result]**

### SHG from squaring E

$$P^{(2)} \propto (E_0 e^{-i\omega t} + c.c.)^2 = E_0^2 e^{-2i\omega t} + 2|E_0|^2 + (E_0^*)^2 e^{+2i\omega t}$$

→ SHG at 2ω, plus DC optical rectification term.

### Symmetry argument for χ⁽²⁾ = 0

If material is centrosymmetric: reversing E should reverse P (same material, opposite direction).
- P⁽²⁾ = ε₀χ⁽²⁾E² → when E → −E: P⁽²⁾ = ε₀χ⁽²⁾(−E)² = +ε₀χ⁽²⁾E² (unchanged, but should be −)
- Contradiction → χ⁽²⁾ must be 0.

## Physical Intuition

Imagine the restoring force as a perfectly symmetric spring: you can pull left or right and get the same response. The induced polarisation is a symmetric function of E. When you take the Fourier transform, only the fundamental and its odd harmonics (3ω, 5ω, …) appear — the even harmonics (2ω, 4ω, …) cancel by symmetry.

For a non-centrosymmetric material (asymmetric spring), the response is different in each direction → even harmonics appear → SHG is possible.

## Common Mistakes

1. Forgetting different units for different orders of χ
2. Missing the c.c. (complex conjugate) terms when squaring E
3. Thinking all materials have χ⁽²⁾ ≠ 0 — only non-centrosymmetric ones
4. Confusing the DC term (optical rectification) with SHG — they come from different terms in E²
